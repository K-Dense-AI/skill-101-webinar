Single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) has transformed our ability to resolve cellular heterogeneity, yet droplet-based protocols remain confounded by ambient RNA contamination and variable capture efficiency. Here we present a probabilistic decontamination framework that jointly models ambient expression profiles and per-cell contamination fractions using a hierarchical Dirichlet-multinomial likelihood. Applied to 14 publicly available datasets (n = 312,455 cells) spanning peripheral blood mononuclear cells, tumor microenvironments, and developing cortex, the method reduced spurious marker co-expression by 41% (95% CI: 36–46%) relative to standard log-normalization, without attenuating bona fide lineage markers. Downstream clustering recovered three previously masked progenitor subpopulations validated against orthogonal CITE-seq protein measurements (Pearson r = 0.83). The approach is computationally tractable (under 20 minutes for 100,000 cells on a single CPU node) and is released as an open-source package with reproducible benchmarks. We anticipate that principled ambient-RNA correction will improve the reliability of rare-cell-type discovery across atlas-scale studies.
